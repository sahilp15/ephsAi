# AP, Honors, and PSEO (College-Level Courses)

EPHS has several ways to take college-level work in high school.

## Advanced Placement (AP)

AP courses are college-level classes taught at the high school. Students may earn
college credit and/or advanced placement at many colleges by earning a qualifying
score on the AP exam given each May.

AP courses offered at EPHS include (confirm current availability and prerequisites on
the Course Selection Hub):

- AP African American Studies
- AP Art History
- AP Studio Art / AP Drawing / AP 3-D Art & Design
- AP Biology
- AP Chemistry
- AP Environmental Science
- AP Physics C: Mechanics
- AP Business
- AP Computer Science A (Java)
- AP Computer Science Principles
- AP AB Calculus and AP BC Calculus
- AP Statistics
- AP English Language
- AP English Literature
- AP Seminar and AP Research (AP Capstone)
- AP Human Geography
- AP U.S. History
- AP World History
- AP U.S. Government & Politics
- AP Comparative Government & Politics
- AP Economics / AP Macroeconomics / AP Microeconomics
- AP Music Theory

## Honors courses

Honors courses offer a faster pace and greater depth. See the Honors section of the
Course Guide for the current list and prerequisites.

## PSEO (Postsecondary Enrollment Options)

PSEO lets eligible students take college courses — often at no cost — for both high
school and college credit. See the PSEO topic and the Course Selection Hub for
eligibility and how credits apply to graduation.

## Articulated college credit

Many career/technical courses offer **articulated college credit** (college credit
earned through the high school course). Look for this noted in the course
descriptions on the Course Selection Hub: https://www.edenpr.org/ephs-courses
