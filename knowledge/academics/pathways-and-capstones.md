# Pathways and Capstone Courses

## Pathways

A **Pathway** is a focused sequence of courses centered on an area of interest. Each
Pathway includes core and elective courses at EPHS, Capstone courses for 11th and
12th graders, and opportunities to earn college credit. Pathways are designed to give
students the experience and skills for their next steps after graduation.

Confirmed examples of Pathways at EPHS include **Finance**, **Entrepreneurship**, and
**Science Research & Design**. There are additional Pathways across career areas — see
the Pathways Overview in the Course Guide and the Course Selection Hub for the full,
current list: https://www.edenpr.org/ephs-courses

## Capstone courses

**Capstone courses** are the culmination of a career pathway. Students apply and
demonstrate their learning in personalized, real-world ways that reflect how
professionals work in that field. Capstones are usually for 11th and 12th graders.

Good to know:

- You can take multiple Capstone courses if you meet the prerequisites.
- You do **not** have to be formally pursuing a Pathway to take a Capstone course.
